Testing CSS pseudo content

Published: June 24, 2020

Application versions

Testing was performed with the following:

  • iOS 13.5.1 / Safari
  • macOS 10.15.5 / Safari 13.1.1
  • Android 10, TalkBack, Chrome 83
  • Windows Firefox 77
  • Windows Chrome 83
  • Windows Edge 83
  • NVDA 2020.1
  • JAWS 2020.2006.12 (June)

Pseudo content and a paragraph

Paragraph text.

Results
VoiceOver + Safari macOS
before/after content announces as part of paragraph.
If navigating into the paragraph (Control + Option + Shift + down arrow) the before/after content will not be read.
VoiceOver + Safari iOS
before/after content can be discovered as individual stops when left/right swiping through content.
Navigating by "words" is awkward and appears to get tripped up by before/after content.
Navigating by "lines" will skip before/after content.
Search by touch will focus before/after content in isolation.
TalkBack + Chrome
before/after content announces as part of paragraph.
Navigating by characters, words or lines, before/after content will be announced.
JAWS + Chrome/Edge
Navigating by line, character, word or sentence announces before/after when announcing paragraph text.
Navigating by "same element" (S) pseudo content will cause JAWS to announce the paragraph 3 times before moving to next "paragraph" in the document.
JAWS + Firefox
Same results as Chrome/Edge.
JAWS & NVDA + IE11
Ignores before/after content as IE11 does not expose it to the accessibility tree.
NVDA + Chrome/Edge
Navigating by line, character, or word announces before/after when announcing announcing paragraph text.
NVDA + Firefox
Same results as Chrome/Edge.

Pseudo content and the dfn element

defining instance of a term

Results
VoiceOver + Safari macOS
before/after content announces as part of the dfn's content.
If navigating into the dfn element, (Control + Option + Shift + down arrow) the before/after content can be navigated to as individual words, just like the child text of the element.
VoiceOver + Safari iOS
before/after content can be discovered as individual stops when left/right swiping through content.
Navigating by "words" is awkward and appears to get tripped up by before/after content.
Navigating by "lines" will skip before/after content.
Search by touch will focus before/after content in isolation.
TalkBack + Chrome
before/after content announces as part of the dfn's content.
Navigating by characters, words or lines, before/after content will be announced.
JAWS + Chrome/Edge
Navigating by line, character, word or sentence announces before/after when announcing dfn text.
Navigating by "same element" (S) pseudo content will cause JAWS to announce the content 3 times before moving to next paragraph in the document (whether the dfn is wrapped in a p or not, it treats the line of text as a paragraph).
JAWS + Firefox
Same results as Chrome.
JAWS & NVDA + IE11
Ignores before/after content as IE11 does not expose it to the accessibility tree.
NVDA + Chrome/Edge
Navigating by line, character, or word announces before/after when announcing announcing dfn text.
NVDA + Firefox
Same results as Chrome/Edge.

Pseudo content with links and buttons

link label

Results
VoiceOver + Safari macOS
before/after content announces as part of the dfn's content.
If navigating into the button or link elements, (Control + Option + Shift + down arrow) the before/after content can be navigated to as individual words, just like the child text of the element.
VoiceOver + Safari iOS
before/after content is announced when focusing button or link.
Navigating by "words" is awkward and appears to get tripped up by before/after content.
Navigating by "lines" will skip before/after content.
Search by touch will focus entire link or button, and not individual before/after or child text of the elements.
TalkBack + Chrome
before/after content is not announced as part of the button or link's accessible name.
Navigating by characters, words or lines, before/after content will not be announced.
JAWS + Chrome/Edge
before/after content not announced in buttons.
Navigating by line, character, word or sentence announces before/after when announcing link text.
JAWS + Firefox
Navigating by line, character, word or sentence announces before/after when announcing link or button text.
Navigating by buttons or form controls will announce before/after content of button.
JAWS & NVDA + IE11
Ignores before/after content as IE11 does not expose it to the accessibility tree.
NVDA + Chrome/Edge
Navigating by line, character, or word announces before/after when announcing button and link text.
Navigating by links, buttons, form controls, before/after is announced.
NVDA + Firefox
Same results as Chrome/Edge.
